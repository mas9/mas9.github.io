Theoretical condensed matter physics

Miguel Ángel Sánchez Martínez

Theoretical condensed matter physicist interested in topological quasiparticles, unconventional superconductivity, and dynamical response functions.

I am currently a Research Associate in the H.H. Wills Physics Laboratory at the University of Bristol. My work lies at the intersection of topological quantum matter, superconductivity, and quasicrystalline systems aimed at connecting theory and experiment.

Research

My research focuses on various aspects of theoretical condensed matter physics, with a particular emphasis on topological systems and unconventional superconductors. A central part of my work is building effective models that capture observables accessible in transport, linear optics, and nonlinear optical experiments.

Current research directions

  • Topological quasiparticles in multifold semimetals
    Linear and nonlinear optical response, circular photogalvanic effect, and anomalous Hall conductivity in chiral crystals hosting multifold fermions.
  • Unconventional superconductivity
    p-wave and d-wave states, chiral and nematic order, acoustic plasmons in multiband systems and their experimental signatures.
  • Topological matter in quasicrystals
    Quasiperiodic Kitaev chains, Majorana modes, and fractal properties of spectra and Green’s functions in aperiodic structures.
  • Dynamical response and dielectric function
    Numerical calculation of Lindhard susceptibilities and dielectric functions in 2D and 3D, including relaxation, finite temperature, and pole tracking in the complex plane.

Methods and tools

  • Green’s function methods and linear response (Kubo) in momentum and real space.
  • Tight-binding Hamiltonians fitted to first-principles band structures.
  • High-precision numerical calculations in Julia, Python, and Mathematica, including Brillouin-zone integration, FFTs, and large-scale diagonalization.
  • Systematic treatment of Kramers–Kronig relations, analytic continuation, and Padé-type techniques for extracting collective modes.
